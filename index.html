Somewhere Between
Every Year We've Known

My dearest friend,

If you're reading this, it means the letter I hid in ink and shadow finally found its way into your hands — and you, patient and clever as ever, found your way into mine.

I wanted this birthday to feel different. Not just another card that gets propped on a shelf for a week and forgotten in a drawer by spring. I wanted you to work for it a little. To hold something up to the light, to turn it over, to wonder. Because that's what you've always done for the people you love — you pay attention. You notice things. So I built you something that asks the same of you, just this once.

I keep thinking about all the ordinary afternoons that somehow became the ones I remember most. The long drives with the windows down and no destination we could name. The conversations that started as nothing and ended three hours later with both of us wondering where the time went. You have a way of making the unremarkable feel important, just by being present for it.

There's a particular kind of luck in having someone who remembers your stories better than you do — who can finish your sentences not because they're impatient, but because they've actually been listening, for years, quietly, the way only real friends bother to.

I don't say this enough, so let me say it plainly, without a joke to hide behind: you have made my life warmer just by being in it. Not louder, not easier — warmer. There's a difference, and you taught it to me.

So here's to another year of terrible puns and late-night calls, of plans we half-make and somehow keep, of being each other's proof that some things don't fade just because time keeps moving. Here's to whatever this next year decides to throw at you — I hope it's mostly good, and I hope wherever I can, I get to stand next to you for it.

Happy birthday. Thank you for finding this. Thank you, more than that, for finding me, years ago, and staying.
